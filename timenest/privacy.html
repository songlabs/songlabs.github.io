TimeNest Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 12, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how TimeNest handles information when you use the app. TimeNest is designed as a local-first calendar and schedule management app, and aims to minimize the collection and transmission of user data.

Information You Create in the App

TimeNest allows you to create and manage calendar events, all-day events, memos, shifts, work-time records, reminders, display settings, language settings, and public-holiday subscription settings.

This information is stored locally in the app sandbox or App Group container on your device by default. TimeNest does not currently provide a TimeNest account system, developer-operated backend, or general-purpose cloud synchronization service.

Shared Calendars and Apple iCloud

When you actively enable a shared calendar, TimeNest uses Apple iCloud and CloudKit to transfer and store the content selected for sharing.

The calendar owner can separately choose whether to share:

  • Calendar events
  • Shift records
  • Work-time records

Recipients have read-only access. They can view shared content, but cannot create, edit, or delete the owner's shared content.

Shared records do not include private memos, notifications, voice-input content, hourly rates, pay amounts, transportation fees, shift templates, advertising state, purchase state, device settings, app settings, Widget-private data, or internal holiday-subscription information.

Shared-calendar availability, invitation processing, storage, and access are handled through Apple iCloud and CloudKit and are also subject to Apple's applicable terms and privacy policies.

Local Storage

TimeNest stores app data such as events, shifts, work records, reminders, display preferences, language settings, holiday subscription choices, and cached holiday information in local app storage.

If you uninstall the app, iOS normally removes the app's local container and the data stored inside it. Data stored through Apple iCloud or CloudKit may be handled separately under Apple's service rules.

Widgets and App Group Data

If TimeNest widgets are enabled, the app may share a limited local calendar snapshot with its Widget Extension through Apple's App Group mechanism.

This exchange occurs locally on the device and is used only to display relevant calendar information in the Widget. It is not a TimeNest-operated cloud synchronization service.

Public-Holiday Subscriptions

TimeNest can download public-holiday data from the holiday source selected in the app's settings. When you test, refresh, or enable a holiday subscription, the app may send HTTPS requests to the selected public ICS provider to download holiday calendar data.

The selected third-party provider may process information such as IP addresses, access logs, or request details according to its own privacy policy. Downloaded holiday data is cached locally on your device for calendar display.

Advertising, Consent, and Tracking

TimeNest uses Google Mobile Ads to display banner advertisements to users who have not purchased the Remove Ads feature.

The Google Mobile Ads SDK may process information such as IP addresses, device identifiers, advertising data, ad interactions, diagnostics, crash information, performance data, and other information required for advertising delivery, measurement, security, and fraud prevention.

TimeNest uses Google User Messaging Platform to manage advertising consent where required. The app may also request authorization through Apple's App Tracking Transparency framework before using information for tracking purposes.

If tracking authorization is denied or restricted, the app's calendar functionality remains available. Depending on the user's choices, location, and applicable rules, Google may provide non-personalized ads, limited ads, or no ads.

TimeNest does not sell user-created calendar data.

In-App Purchases

TimeNest provides a one-time Remove Ads purchase through Apple In-App Purchase and StoreKit.

Purchase processing and payment information are handled by Apple. TimeNest does not collect or store payment card details. Purchase restoration uses transaction and entitlement information provided by Apple StoreKit.

Users with an active Remove Ads entitlement do not see banner advertisements.

Third-Party Services

TimeNest may interact with the following third-party services depending on the features you use:

  • Apple iCloud and CloudKit for optional shared calendars
  • Apple StoreKit for the Remove Ads in-app purchase
  • Google Mobile Ads and Google User Messaging Platform for advertising and consent
  • Public ICS holiday providers selected in the app's settings

These services may process data under their own terms and privacy policies.

Data Deletion and Shared-Calendar Access

You can delete events, shifts, work records, and other content that you created in TimeNest from within the app.

When a shared-calendar owner deletes shared content, it is removed from the owner's local data and from recipients' shared views during a later synchronization.

The owner can stop sharing from the Shared Calendar management screen. This revokes recipient access without deleting the owner's remaining local data.

A recipient can leave a shared calendar. TimeNest then removes the shared calendar's local entry and cache from the recipient's device without deleting the owner's data.

Holiday subscriptions can be disabled or modified in Settings. Uninstalling the app normally removes locally stored app data from the device.

Children's Privacy

TimeNest is not designed to knowingly collect personal information from children. The app does not currently provide account registration or developer-operated cloud storage.

Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated when TimeNest changes its features, data handling, third-party services, advertising configuration, or legal requirements. The latest version will be posted on this page.
